cliffs in the world -- 被世界上最高的悬崖包围。 and Father Damien, 还有为夏威夷社区奉献一生的比利时传教士 the Belgian missionary who gave his life for the Hawaiian community. ——达米安神父的故事。 As a young nurse, 我的姑姑，作为一名年轻的护士， my aunt trained the nuns caring for the remaining lepers 在达米安神父死于麻风病近一百年后， almost a 100 years after Father Damien died of leprosy. 训练修女们照顾剩下的麻风病患者。 I remember stories she told 我依旧记得她讲述的故事： about traveling down switchback cliff paths on a mule, 她和我的叔叔骑着骡子穿行在高低起伏的悬崖小路上。 while my uncle played her favorite hula songs on the ukulele 我的叔叔用尤克里里琴弹奏她最喜欢的夏威夷歌曲， all the way down to Kalaupapa. 直到到达卡劳帕帕。 You see, as a youngster, 作为一个儿童， I was always curious about a few things. 我一直对一些事情感到好奇。 First was why a Belgian missionary chose to live in complete isolation in Kalaupapa, 首先，为什么一个比利时传教士要选择在卡劳帕帕住着与世隔绝的生活， knowing he would inevitably contract leprosy from the community of people he sought to help. 并且不可避免会从他帮助的社区里的人们那里传染到麻风病。 And secondly, 还有， where did the leprosy bacteria come from? 卡劳帕帕的麻风病菌是哪里来的？ And why were Kānaka Maoli, 为什么夏威夷的土著居民 the indigenous people of Hawaii, 如此容易受到麻风病的感染？ so susceptible to developing leprosy, or "mai Pake?" 或者 “麻风病”(夏威夷语)。 This got my curious about what makes us unique as Hawaiians -- 这让我好奇是什么让我们夏威夷人不同？ namely, our genetic makeup. 答案就是我们的基因。 But it wasn't until high school, 但是直到高中， through the Human Genome Project, 通过一个人类基因组的项目， that I realized I wasn't alone 我才发现我并不是唯一一个 in trying to connect our unique genetic ancestry 想要追溯我们特有的基因祖先， to our potential health, wellness and illness. 为了了解我们的潜在健康和疾病。 You see, 要知道， the 2.7 billion-dollar project 这个27亿美元的计划 promised an era of predictive and preventative medicine 在研究我们特有基因的基础上 based on our unique genetic makeup. 保证了一个可预测，可预防的医疗时代。 So to me it always seemed obvious 显而易见， that in order to achieve this dream, 为了要完成这一个梦想， we would need to sequence a diverse cohort of people 我们需要排列一群不同的人 to obtain the full spectrum of human genetic variation on the planet. 来取得地球上变化多样的人类基因的遗传谱。 That's why 10 years later, 这就是为什么10年后， it continues to shock me, 我不断被惊讶， knowing that 96 percent of genome studies associating common genetic variation with specific diseases 发现96%与特定疾病有关的基因多样性的实验 have focused exclusively on individuals of European ancestry. 都只研究了祖先是欧洲人的人群。 Now you don't need a PhD 你不需要一个博士学位都能发现 to see that that leaves four percent for the rest of diversity. 其他的种族只有4%。 And in my own is the Human Genome Project actually for? 人类基因组计划究竟是为了谁？ Just like we have different colored eyes and hair, 就像我们有着不同颜色的眼睛和头发一样， we metabolize drugs differently 根据我们的基因组 based on the variation in our 也是专门为欧洲血统的人。 This bias 这一不公平 and systematic lack of engagement of indigenous people 和缺少对土著居民的系统研究 in both clinical trials and genome studies 在医疗临床和基因研究领域 is partially the result of a history of
